# TOP Interview

## What are your thoughts on reaching the 25th anniversary?

It has been 25 years that seemed long yet short. Looking back, I have accumulated so many memories that I'm happy to recall. I had confidence. I am grateful for all experiences and face each new challenge one by one, though I'm still not satisfied in my heart.

*"It's been 25 years that seemed long yet short, but I'm still not satisfied"*
- Representative Director: Hiroyuki Sugiura

## What triggered the establishment of the company and what experiences led to it?

*"It started from the desire to 'see the next work'"*

Personally, I've always had the desire to see new works. As we proceeded with projects in the studio, the desire to see the next work grew stronger.

## What events are particularly memorable?

*"The production of the 'Xenoblade Series' was deeply moving"*

It was one of the goals I had been thinking about since the company's establishment, and it was deeply moving to realize it after various challenges.

## Has the company grown smoothly from establishment until now?

*"There were crises in the early days, but we overcame them with passion"*

We proceeded with passionate feelings many times, and that became the power to overcome difficulties.

## Page 10: Recruitment Guide - Planners and Programmers

# What kind of work does a planner do?

## We constantly think of new "play" and seek the optimal way to realize it.

**Propose and create "play" that makes games interesting**
- The main task is to think of "play" that makes games interesting, summarize it in a proposal, and present it.

**Bring together each element, create a proposal and share it**
- After deciding on the game concept, we summarize elements like battle systems and character settings in a proposal and share them.

**Examples of planner responsibilities**
- [Map] Think about what is produced on the map and the routes players move along.
- [Battle] Think about the battle system proposal and implement it in cooperation with programmers.
- [Quest] Think about the content of quests players experience and create the game's worldview.

# What kind of work does a programmer do?

## We handle a wide range of tasks from data implementation to environment preparation.

**Design and implement programs that make games work**
- We play the role of making games work through programs, such as character movements and screen displays.

**Quickly respond to unexpected bugs**
- We maintain game quality by finding program bugs and fixing them quickly.

**Also responsible for creating tools to support problem-solving**
- We create tools to solve various problems in game development and support development.

## INTERVIEW: We persistently pursue the "fun" of games.

**What is your main job content?**
- I'm in charge of tasks to pursue the "fun" of the entire game, such as game planning, scenario design, and level design.

**When do you feel fulfillment?**
- I feel fulfillment when new ideas take shape or when I get responses from players.

**What do you value in your work?**
- I value constantly thinking of new "play" and seeking the optimal way to realize it.

**What are your future goals?**
- I aim to incorporate new technologies and further evolve gameplay.

## INTERVIEW: Using high-level technology and persistently improving quality.

**What is your main job content?**
- To realize the ideas planners think of, I design and implement programs. I use languages like C++ and Python.

**When do you feel fulfillment?**
- I feel fulfillment when I improve game quality using various technologies such as AI and physics calculations.

**What do you value in your work?**
- I value improving quality so that the released game can be enjoyed by many people.

**What are your future goals?**
- I aim to actively learn new technologies and create higher quality games.
