Today, we're going to talk about the Ridge Racer 3D game, of course, but I'd also like to talk about a number of other things, including what serves as the backbone for you in your work.SakagamiIt's my pleasure to be here.IwataBefore you joined NAMCO (now NAMCO BANDAI Games Inc.)1, when and how did you first encounter video games? 1. NAMCO (now NAMCO BANDAI Games Inc.): A company founded in 1955 that develops video game software and operates video arcades. In 2006, it merged with the video game development division of BANDAI Co., Ltd. and restarted under the name NAMCO BANDAI Games Inc. SakagamiI first encountered video games at a video game arcade. But I had always loved movies. My house was in Nishinomiya in Hyogo Prefecture, but I would go all the way to Osaka to see movies.IwataAbout how old were you then?SakagamiThat started about when I was in the fifth grade of elementary school and continued until about my third year of high school. My school had a rule that elementary and junior high students couldn't go from Nishinomiya to Osaka for fun, but my father was running a sushi restaurant in Osaka, so I could go if I said I was going to help out at the restaurant. I saw Jaws2 once, and that made me love movies, so I started going to the theater all the time, without even bothering to check the movie times. 2. Jaws: An American movie about a giant man-eating shark. Directed by Steven Spielberg and released in 1975. IwataReally? You went to the movie theater without even checking when the movies were going to start?SakagamiYeah. My father had shareholders' complimentary tickets from a movie company, and they only had the names on them of certain movie theaters where they were valid.IwataSo as long as you went to the movie theater, you were able to watch movies.SakagamiRight. Most of the time, the movie had already started when I got there, but I didn't like to start watching partway through, so I'd go kill time at a video arcade.IwataAh, I see. So that's how this connects to video games. (laughs)SakagamiYes. Sorry for the huge digression! (laughs) But that's how I encountered video games.IwataWhat kinds of games did you play while waiting for the movies?SakagamiI was obsessed with Xevious.3 But I wasn't any good at first, so the game would be over right away. The arcade game appeared in February 1983, and the Famicom (NES™) version appeared in November 1984. IwataYou got to where you could play for a whole hour on just 100 yen. Would you watch just any kind of movie?SakagamiI was omnivorous. After all, it depended on the tickets! (laughs)IwataOh, right. You couldn't choose what you watched. (laughs)SakagamiRight (laughs), so I watched a lot of B movies!IwataI imagine, however, that watching all those movies proved helpful later on.SakagamiYes, very helpful. I saw lots of movies, without being picky, so I absorbed the elements of a wide variety of genres.IwataSo, while you learned the appeal of video games, you watched lots of movies and became enamored of their appeal, so it's no wonder you went into the film industry.SakagamiExactly.
