Overtaken by New Super Mario Bros. 2Iwata
Today, I will be asking about New Super Luigi U. Director Takemoto-san spent three years developing New Super Mario Bros. U1, which came out last December, and he continued on making this one without a break.
1. New Super Mario Bros. U: An action game released simultaneously with the Wii U console in November 2012.
Takemoto
Yes. We made New Super Mario Bros. U to go along with the launch of the Wii U console, and together with Tezuka-san and those guys began by thinking of New Super Luigi U as downloadable content.2
2. Downloadable content: Digital downloads of New Super Luigi U began June 19, 2013 as additional, downloadable content for people who own New Super Mario Bros. U. A packaged version for people who do not have New Super Mario Bros. U also became available on Aug. 25, 2013.
Iwata
In the end, the content turned out to be such that you could say you made a whole new game rather than making a downloadable content.
Takemoto
Yes, you could.
Tezuka
I was involved with New Super Mario Bros. 23 for the Nintendo 3DS system, which came out before New Super Mario Bros. U, so I feel like I've done nothing but make Mario games! (laughs)
3. New Super Mario Bros. 2: An action game released for the Nintendo 3DS system in August 2012.
Iwata
But Mario doesn't show up this time! (laughs)
Tezuka
Oh, right. (laughs)
Iwata
Can you say that in a way, this development involved thinking for a long time about what downloadable content should be like for Super Mario?
Tezuka
That's right. I never thought that we would think this deeply about downloadable content.
Iwata
What was it like as you got started?
Tezuka
We didn't have such a big job planned at first. We began by thinking about expanding secondary play such as we did for New Super Mario Bros. 2 for the Nintendo 3DS. For example, we considered adding a lot of Boost Rush Mode courses.
Iwata
For New Super Mario Bros. 2, you can play additional packs4 of special Coin Rush Mode courses, and you thought of the same thing for New Super Mario Bros. U.
4. Additional packs: For-pay collections of special courses for Coin Rush Mode. Introduced in four stages, there are a total of ten packs. For more information about  downloadable content packs for New Super Mario Bros. 2, click here.
Tezuka
Right. That wasn't by express agreement, it just happened by chance. As I remember it, we had decided to distribute additional courses for the two titles, but each group was going to think about what they should be like for each game, and the ideas for this one came up just a little bit faster than for New Super Mario Bros. 2.
Iwata
New Super Mario Bros. U was faster?
Tezuka
Yes. Later, the idea of additional packs for Coin Rush Mode came up from the New Super Mario Bros. 2 team, and we moved forward with that because that game was being released first. However, as we were working on it, the sameness of the idea began to bother me, so I started thinking we should come up with a different idea for New Super Mario Bros. U.
Iwata
Takemoto-san, did it not quite sit right with you (laughs) when you had thought of this great idea, but then someone who came along later seized the lead?
Takemoto
Well, that game was coming out first, so it couldn't be helped. (laughs)
Iwata
That's very big of you! (laughs)
Takemoto
Oh not at all! (laughs) I was a little worried that some might wonder why the downloadable content wasn't in the main game from the start, but I thought positively about itâ"If they're doing that, then we should come up with a better idea."
Iwata
We began development of the additional packs for New Super Mario Bros. 2 after the original game was complete and we had analyzed how people were playing it. Our intention was to prevent any sense of the original game being slim in content because there was going to be downloadable content, but nonetheless, some did wonder why it wasn't in there from the start.
Takemoto
Yeah. And personally, I'd never quite taken to the idea of adding Boost Rush Mode courses, so we decided to consider whether there were any other good approaches.
Tezuka
Then we began experimenting and made something for New Super Mario Bros. U that had extremely short courses but increased difficulty.
Iwata
And that led "directly" to New Super Luigi U.
Tezuka
Yes.
Iwata
Why did you decide to shorten the courses but make them more difficult?
Tezuka
We originally assumed the downloadable content would be for people who had played New Super Mario Bros. U, and decided to make it for people who wanted to play it even more. So I had been saying it could be a little more difficult.
Iwata
You had advanced gamers in mind.
Tezuka
Yes. But it would be exhausting to play long, difficult courses.
Iwata
You would get really tired.
Tezuka
So I thought it was necessary to shorten them.
Iwata
In other words, if gameplay is more intense and exciting, you can't keep up that level of tension for a long time, so you made them shorter.
Tezuka
Yes, that's right.
Takemoto
I thought about it a little differently.
Iwata
How so?
Takemoto
The New Super Mario Bros. series has come out sequentially for the Nintendo DS, Wii, Nintendo 3DS and Wii U, and as it went along, the courses kept getting longer.
Iwata
The courses have gradually gotten longer?
Takemoto
Yes. I don't think the players would have noticed anything unusual, but if you compare them, yes. It was gradual, but they've become longer.
Iwata
Oh, I see.
Takemoto
So in making the downloadable content this time, I decided to play back through the Super Mario series. And when I played Super Mario Bros. 35, it felt really short!
5. Super Mario Bros. 3: An action game released for the NES system in October 1988.
Iwata
You mean the actual length of the courses?
Takemoto
Yes. I cleared them way back when without ever thinking they were short.
Iwata
That's true for me as well.
Takemoto
But when I play them now, I'm like, "Huh? It's over already?" But they feel so comfortable to play. So while I thought that the players might feel as if short courses might lack something, I thought a length that allowed you to see the goal coming up once you got over the hump would serve as motivation to play over and over.
Iwata
On a short course, if you mess up, you can see the goal right ahead, so you feel like giving it another try.
Takemoto
Right. So just when I was thinking that I wanted short courses for the downloadable content this time, Tezuka-san just happened to suggest trying short courses, so the timing was perfect.
Tezuka
By chance. (laughs)
Takemoto
Yeah. (laughs)
